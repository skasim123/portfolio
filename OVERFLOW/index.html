Proceed to Projects

Trash-Throwing
Trebuchet


In this project, we wanted to create a machine that consisted of at least a minimum of 4 simple mechanisms, in order to make an aspect of our life easier. We decided to create a machine that throws away trash for us. As the group project designer, my personal responsibility was to design, and build a prototype that illustrated our brainstorming ideas that we all collaborated on. We learned that in a trebuchet, the higher the effort force, the stronger the output force would be. This meant that we had to add more weights in order to propel our projectile (trash) further. This project would have been very difficult if it wasn't for our collaborative brainstorming efforts. I had to turn our ideas into a physical prototype, which helped me create very detailed sketches to aid me to build the final machine.

Bridge
Design


This project was executed in my class Principles of Engineering. In this project, we were instructed to create a feasible design for a cost-efficient and durable bridge. This task was assigned by our client, The American Bridge Compan y. Our bridge consisted of many materials, but the most common material that we used was the Carbon Steel, due to its ductile properties, and its extremely low cost. This project required a significant amount of teamwork and communication/collaboration between the members, in order to create this bridge. We initially wanted to use my bridge alone for the final solution, but we had decided, that if we fused all the crucial aspects of our designs, we would have a very well designed outcome. When our bridge was tested, we were able to create a very cheap bridge, and had one of the top 3 bridges in our class. All of this could not have been accomplished without the knowledge of how trusse s worked, and how different forces react with each other. We showed our mastery of these skills during this strenuous assignment.
